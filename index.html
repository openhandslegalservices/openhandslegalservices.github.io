The law is a powerful tool against poverty. Open Hands Legal Services brings it directly to low-income and homeless New Yorkers at soup kitchens and shelters across the city.

We were founded in 2009 by attorneys called to use their skills to serve the city.

Recognizing that many problems faced by the poor and the oppressed could be solved by an attorney, the group began volunteering at the Father’s Heart, an East Village soup kitchen.

Since then, we’ve grown dramatically in response to demand from other soup kitchens and shelters, including the Bowery Mission, New York City Rescue Mission, and Exodus Transitional Community.

"Elsa"

Elsa lives in Harlem in a rent-controlled apartment. As rents around her increase, so does pressure to leave.

Elsa's landlord has failed to complete serious repairs, even though the Department of Buildings mandated them years ago. Her landlord also wrongfully combined her electricity box with another apartment so that she was left with a bill she couldn't pay.

Elsa's case is typical of the harassment that vulnerable tenants experience. in many cases, behavior like this is unchecked because tenants cannot afford legal consultation.

Already past the age of retirement, and living in a city where affordable housing is hard to find, it’s essential that Elsa can stay in her home.

Elsa came to meet with us at the Community Hope Center in East Harlem, where Candace Chung conducts intake every week.

Candace started volunteering in 2013 and has become one of our most loyal supporters. She’s dedicated thousands of hours to our clients, not only at the Community Hope Center, but also at the Father’s Heart in East Village, the Relief Bus in East Harlem, and the New York City Rescue Mission in Lower Manhattan.

She is one of the many volunteers who helps Open Hands meet clients right where they are.

We’re working with Elsa to correct her erroneous bill and to deal with the $1,000 of wrongful debt she’s incurred. Elsa can rest assured that her electricity will not be shut off.

We're advising Elsa on how to get the repairs to which she's entitled.

Surrounded by advocates, she no longer feels pressured or intimidated.
